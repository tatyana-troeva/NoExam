Income Replacement Summary

Assuming $InflationRate$% inflation and $InterestRate$% earnings it requires $$ReplacedFaceValue$ to provide an annual indexed income of $$AnnualIndexedIncome$.

In the first year, $$AnnualIndexedIncome$ of income would be taken from the $$ReplacedFaceValue$ of initial capital. The $$RemainingAfterYear1$ of capital left to invest would earn $$RemainingYear1Interest$ providing $$RemainingYear1WithInterest$ of initial capital for the second year. In the second year, $$SecondYearAnnualIndexedIncome$ of income would be taken as income leaving $$RemainingAfterYear2$ to be invested.

$$SecondYearAnnualIndexedIncome$ in the 2nd year has the same purchasing power as $$AnnualIndexedIncome$ in the 1st year ($InflationRate$% inflation).

In the following schedule each year's income is indexed to keep pace with inflation. After $NumYearsReplacementIncomeRequiredFor$ years there is just enough left for the final year's income.
